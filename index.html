  • why I choice this job

    When I was in college, China was in a period of economic transition, advocating the strategy of strengthening the country through science and technology. The government and enterprises were increasingly encouraging and emphasizing innovation, and many companies emerged in China that relied on technological innovation to capture the product market, such as Huawei, ZTE, DJI, and others. Meanwhile, China's intellectual property protection industry has seen a leap forward in this context, and I keenly observed this phenomenon. In 2018, I saw in the news that the U.S. federal court ruled on the Apple lawsuit against Samsung for patent infringement, ruling that Samsung Electronics infringed on Apple's design and other technology patents for iPhone products in 2012, and Samsung eventually had to pay $539 million in royalties to Apple. Apple and Samsung have been fighting patent wars worldwide since 2011, and the time consumed and money invested is enormous, making me realize the importance of patent protection for technology innovation companies. With the progress and development of technology, patent wars similar to Apple and Samsung's will undoubtedly continue to emerge in the future. The demand for high-end talents in patent protection will also increase, which is an excellent employment opportunity for practitioners. I entered the IP protection industry, hoping to become a top expert.

  • what I get from this job

The patent examination work gives me a deeper understanding of China's patent examination and protection system and other countries worldwide.
